# Introduction

The genesis of this project is the Traveller Wiki. The wiki has collected the ideas and writings of Traveller authors from more than forty years. It now contains more than two hundred articles for beasts and more than five hundred sophonts.
We conceived the beasts and sophonts project to update each of the articles with a consistent account. When the Traveller 5th edition rules were published, it provided the most detailed manner of describing both beasts and sophonts of any edition of Traveller.
The Traveller 5th edition rules contain 10 makers (Star systems, Starships, Vehicles, Weapons, Armor, Things, Robots, Beasts, Sophonts, and Characters), each a collection of tables and rules to produce elements of the Traveller universe. This book consists of the BeastMaker and Sophont Creation System rules. The rules are reorganized; some of the rules for sophont creation are moved into the BeastMaker section with all known er-rata from the core rules applied. Definitions are expanded, and more examples are provided to clarify sections of the generation process.

Chapter 1, **BeastMaker Simple** is a quick system for generating beasts. The result is a one-line description with enough information for an encounter with each creature. The whole system creates a table with a selection of encounter possibilities.
Chapter 2, **BeastMaker Extended** generates detailed information about a single creature. These optional steps create additional background for beasts and core data for sophonts. This includes life cycle and senses.
Chapter 3, **Sophont Creation** takes the detailed description from the BeastMaker rules and extends them further for de-scribing a Sophont, ready for use by referees and players as new characters.

# BeastMaker Simple

This chapter contains the rules for generating a basic out-line of one or more creatures. It may be useful to have several pre-generated encounter tables on hand for common terrain types. If the referee knows a specific beast will be required, then it should be generated. However, information about beasts can be created quickly and easily, and you do not need to build extensive Beast Encounter Tables in advance.

The BeastMaker and sophont creation process is expressed as a dice-driven random process, but it can also be used as a deliberate system where you select elements to achieve specific results.

### Beast Encounter Tables

Beast encounters are governed by a series of Beast Encounter Tables - one for each terrain type on a world. As players move through a terrain hex, an encounter may occur. When it does, the Beast Encounter Table indicates what specific animal type is present. The table entry pro-vides details for a specific animal (or animals) and how they might react to the encounter.

### What Beasts Are Encountered

Beasts in any ecological system interact with each other, forming food chains, obeying instincts, defending territory, and generally living out their lives. When people enter one of these ecological systems, they may encounter these animals: sometimes merely seeing them in the distance, at other times confronting them up close.

The Beast Encounter Tables concern themselves with important animals that provide challenges and (potentially) interesting encounters. The tables usually ignore small, in-consequential animals: mice and squirrels, or their ecological counterparts, may inhabit the terrain hex, but unless they have some importance, they do not appear on the Beast Encounter Tables.

The simple table is adapted to most circumstances, resolving animal type with 1D. The animals that appear may be a nuisance, may delay a journey, or may simply show the types of animals that inhabit this world. When an encounter occurs, roll 1D on the table to determine the specific encounter.

## Homeworld

The homeworld of each beast (or sophont) determines many details about the creature. The specific details of the homeworld include primary star, any secondary stars, and orbit location. These define the overall planetary climate and the basis of visual senses.

The planet Size, Atmosphere, and Hydrographics determine the terrain, climate, and food chain. Some combinations of Size and Atmosphere will preclude the generation           of beasts. The simple rules assume you are using a world already created by the system generation rules (see Core rules book 3 p. 25), or a world from an existing data set.

## Terrain

Beasts evolve and adapt to a specific terrain. Mapping a world determines the specific terrains available Terrain types are described in Core rules Book 3 p. 78. If the beast’s homeworld has a map, use the terrain types on the map to generate the Beast Encounter Table. If you do not have a map, select or roll for the terrain on the Native Terrain and Locomotion table. See also map generation on Core rules Book 3 p. 49.

When you know the terrain type, roll 1D on the Native Terrain table to determine the locomotion type, consulting the line for the terrain where your creature lives.

### Types of Locomotion

The way an animal moves is fundamental to an understanding of its activities and behavior. There are several basic types of locomotion:
**Walkers** are animals that move on land which include movement like climbing, jumping, hopping, crawling, or unusual methods like slithering or flowing.
**Swimmers** are animals that live and move in water (or the fluid of the world) at or near the surface, occasionally moving across the surface layer. Swimmers may breathe air or water (or both). Swimming contrasts with Diving.
**Divers** live, breathe, and move in water (or the fluid of the world), primarily in the depths.
**Flyers** are animals capable of in the atmosphere. Flying includes gliding, lighter-than-atmosphere buoyancy, kiting, and other types of flight.
**Drifters** are animals moved by outside forces such as winds, storms, streams, rivers, flooding, or other movement of the environment. Like Static, except not rooted to one spot.
**Static** animals do not normally move, rooted to one spot.

### Multiple Locomotion Types

An animal may use multiple types of locomotion:
**Amphibians** can move on land and in water. It can walk and swim. For example, a frog or a crocodile.
**Aquatic** animals operate in a water environment both swimming and diving. For example, a whale.
**Flyers** are assumed to be able to both walk and fly. In theory a Flyer would be an animal that flies exclusively. For example, a locust, or a wren.
**Triphibian** is an animal that can move on land, in water, and can fly. For example, a duck.
**Flyphib** is an animal that can both swim and fly. For example, a flying fish.

## Types

Beasts are identified (and classified) in terms of the ecological niches they occupy. Each beast occupies a specific position in the hierarchical food chain.
A beast that eats grass occupies a niche in the environment; a beast that eats grazers is another part of the food chain and occupies a different (and equally essential) niche. Although each niche is usually filled by one species, many different species can fill that niche
Select the niche by the row of the Beast Encounters table being filled. If not filling an encounter table, you may select or roll flux for the niche. Roll flux to determine the specific strategy.

### Producers

Organisms that produce food are producers. In most cases, producers are plants, but producers can be beasts as well. There are two types of producers:
**Baskers** passively acquire nutrients and energy benefiting from the natural presence of nutrients They are typically static or slow moving. A tree is a common example.
**Collectors** actively acquire nutrients and energy, adding an element of movement to their metabolic processes: they may move slowly to follow veins or currents of nutrients; they may move to face the sun or to follow favorable environmental changes. An example of a collector is a sunflower.

### Herbivores

Herbivores eat unresisting food. While this usually means planteaters, the definition here includes the eating of unresisting animals as well. Herbivores are of three types:
**Grazers** devote most of their time to eating. They may be solitary or grouped in herds. Their primary defense is flight, although such action may result in stampedes, which could endanger travellers who get in their path. When forced to, they will fight fiercely until killed or routed. A typical Terran grazer is the antelope. The whale, consuming krill, is also a grazer.
**Intermittents** do not devote full time to eating. Intermittents usually “freeze” when an encounter occurs, fleeing if attacked by a larger animal. An intermittent may attack to protect territory or young. Typical Terran intermittents are the chipmunk and the elephant.
**Filters** pass the environment through their bodies. Unlike grazers, which move to eat food, filters move a flow of water or air through themselves to acquire food. Generally, filters suck, trip, push, or pull anything at close range into a digestive sac. A filter can absorb a creature up to twice its own size. Filters are solitary and usually slow moving if they move at all. Terran filters are generally aquatic, such as the barnacle.

### Omnivores

Omnivores eat food without regard to its resistance. The bear, which eats fruits and berries as readily as it hunts for animals, is an omnivore. Omnivores have three types.
**Gatherers** display a greater tendency toward herbivorous behavior. In most respects, they are like herbivore intermittents. Typical Terran gatherers are the raccoon and the chimpanzee.
**Hunters** display a greater tendency toward carnivorous behavior. In most respects, they are like small or inefficient carnivore chasers. Typical Terran hunters are bears or humans.
**Hunter/Gatherers** (H/G) are animals that blend the characteristics of both hunter and gatherer with a balanced diet.
**Eaters** are a true omnivore, eating anything and everything without distinguishing its food and consumes all that it confronts. Eaters present considerable danger since they do not avoid anything when encountered. A typical Terran eater is the army ant (when an entire swarm is considered one organism).

### Carnivores

Carnivores prey on other animals by attacking and killing them in the face of resistance and are of five types.
**Pouncers** kill their prey by attacking from hiding or by stalking and springing. Typical Terran pouncers are cats.
**Chasers** kill their prey by attacking after a chase. They tend to be pack animals. Typical Terran chasers are wolves.
**Trappers** passively allow their prey to enter a created trap wherein they are killed and eaten. Trappers tend to be solitary and slow but will attack any animal that enters their trap. Usually, a trap will not wound or damage the trapped creature but will tend to hold it for the trapper to attack. A typical Terran trapper is the spider.
**Sirens** create a lure to draw prey to the animal or a trap, created in much the same manner as the Trapper. In most cases, the lure will be specific to some animal but will be unnoticed by sophonts. In rare cases, the lure will be universal, perhaps a smell or scent, a mirage, or a beautiful configuration, which will attract characters into a vulnerable position. Very rarely, the lure will be psionic. Typical Terran sirens are the anglerfish (its mouth is the trap) and the Venus flytrap.
**Killers** devote much attention to killing, apparently for the act itself, in a kind of blood lust. Killers’ reason (such as territorial defense) is replaced by a raw killing instinct. Attacks by killers are fierce and violent and they generally disregard the defender’s size as a factor. A typical Terran killer is the shark.

### Scavengers

Scavengers share or steal the prey of others or take the remains of kills. Scavengers are of four types.
**Intimidators** establish their claim to food by frightening or threatening other animals. The standard procedure is to approach a kill and force other away by appearing to be a threat. A typical Terran intimidator is the jackal.
**Hijackers** establish their claim to food by simply taking it. They rely on their superior strength or size to allow them to hijack food because the other animals present cannot effectively object. A typical Terran hijacker is the lion or the Tyrannosaurus Rex.
**CarrionEaters** take meat when it becomes available (often waiting patiently for all other threats to disperse before beginning). A typical Terran carrion-eater is the buzzard.
**Reducers** act constantly on all available food. They reduce the remains after all other scavengers are finished with it by consuming bone and other leavings. Terran reducers are all microscopic, such as bacteria.

### Autotrophs

Autotrophs (self-feeding) creatures create their own food supply from an external energy source and available nutrients rather than consuming other life forms. There are five types of producers based upon the source of energy used in their feeding process.
**Photosynthetic**: Light of specific colors drive the chemical processes. This is the most common energy source and the default for producers.
**Chemosynthetic**: Chemical reactions from energetic compounds drive the creation of internal food processes. Terran examples include bacteria in hostile environments like deep sea vents and hot springs.
**Electrosynthetic**: A weak electrical current flow drives the chemical processes creating the basic chemicals for life. The common form is bacteria creating the final connection in a galvanic cell.
**Radiosynthetic**: Radioactive decay creates the energy to power chemical processes.
**Plasmosynthetic**: High energy plasma, usually so-lar winds and cosmic rays are the energy source. These life forms are either airborne or vacuum dwelling to access the energy to power their internal processes.

## Reactions

Beasts react to encounters with people in three basic ways: they may attack, they may flee, or they may do nothing. The reaction roll provides a way to determine which reaction occurs.
Reaction rolls are individualized for each beast. The Reactions from the Reactions table specify the base values for Attack and Flee. Subtract 1D from the values. For example, the Reducer attack value is 12, the result may be from 7 to 11.
Some animals will attack If Possible (P): they attack if their weapon is in range (Range 1).
Some animals will attack if they have Surprise: they attack if an individual is within 5 meters (Range 1).
Some animals will flee if they are Surprised: they flee if an individual is within 5 meters (Range 1).
Most animals roll for Attack. If they do not Attack, they roll for Flee. If they do not flee, they remain in place. Herbivores roll for Flee first. If they do not flee, they roll for Attack. If they do not Attack, they remain in place.

### Definition of Creature Sizes

Biologists divide vitae (living things) into four size categories:
**MicroVitae** are the smallest of the size divisions, in-cluding mainly microorganisms but also sometimes applied to the tiniest species of animal groups such as insects. Smaller than 0.1mm in size.
**MesoVitae** are 0.1 to 2mm in size. Beast of this size are insects, reducers, and producers.
**MacroVitae** are defined as larger than 2mm in size. Beasts of this size mass between a few micrograms to around 10kg.
**MegaVitae** are creatures a half a meter or more in their longest dimension and massing over 10kg. This size is generally regarded as the minimum size for sophonts.

The expected Speed the animal can achieve. Speed uses the same scale as vehicles. See the Vehicle Speed chart (Core rules Book 3 p. 147) and the Impact Damage Benchmark chart (Core rules Book 1 p. 37).

DM+1 for swimmers, DM+2 for divers, DM+2 for Flyers. DM5 for Producers. Beasts with a locomotion of static always have a Speed of static. Beasts with other locomotion types but a Speed of static may be capable of slow movement. Beasts with
multiple forms of locomotion will have two or three different speeds, one for each mode.
Conservative Speed (SpeedC) is an energy-conserving movement rate, the human equivalent of walking. Beasts can maintain this pace for an hour per point of End. Sophonts with Vigor or Stamina can manage half an hour per point.
Beasts use Attack Speed or Fleeing Speed (SpeedAF) while in these situations. This pace can be maintained for one minute or combat round per point of End, checking against End to maintain it.
Beasts use Burst Speed (SpeedB) is an extraordinary sprinting ability. Beasts make checks against Vigor (1/2 End or Sta) each round to maintain this pace.

## Weapon

Each creature has one primary method of attacking other creatures or defending itself from attacks. Each of these forms of attack does a type of damage from the damage type table (Core Rules Book 1 p. 211). The weapon description is, in some cases, left vague because it depends upon the exact body structure. The detailed body structure is generated in chapter 2.

Determine the weapon by rolling flux on the Weapon table. DM 1 if a Carnivore, DM +1 if an Herbivore.

**Body** means the creature uses its whole body to inflict blows by ramming or slamming into their opponent.
**Claws** are sharp, reinforced structures at the end of feet or manipulators used to catch, hold, or rend other creatures. Claws inflict cutting damage.
**Horns** or **Antlers** are a structure on the head. They may be simple straight growths, curved, with or without branches. Some beasts shed and regrow their horns; others keep one set permanently. These inflict cutting damage.
**Peds** are the foot or feet of a other body structures like manipulators, a proboscis, or a tail. These do blow damage.
**Spikes** or **Quills** are hardened body structures with sharp, sometimes barbed, points. Primarily defensive, the spikes serve to deter other attackers. Quills are a form of spikes that detach easily from the creature’s body. Spikes and quills inflict cutting damage.
**Stingers** are hardened body structures with an attached source of venom. Unlike spikes or quills, stingers are for attacking rather than a defensive structure. Stingers inflict cutting damage and may add either Tranq or Poison.
**Teeth** are reinforced structures in the mouth used to break down food. Carnivores use them for hunting and defensive purposes. Teeth inflict cutting damage.
**Tusks**, **Fangs** or a **Beak** are elongated teeth or similar structures around the mouth of the animal, protruding well outside of the mouth. These inflict cutting damage.

## Beasts in Combat

Beasts in combat are treated like characters for attacking. Beasts have a Melee number equal to their rolled Strength. Damage is equal to their strength, and type determined by their natural weapon type. Beasts using their body as a weapon use the impact rules to determine success and damage.
Beasts take damage in the way NPCs do. For creatures of size 3 or smaller any damage will take them out of action (OOA). For beasts of size 4 three or more hits will take them OOA. Beasts of size 5 require 12+ hits, size 6 require 35+ hits, and size 7 require 100+ hits.

# BeastMaker Simple Example

For our worked example, we select the world of Empire/Tobia (Trojan Reach 2711) \[B679334C G2 V M4 V]. For this world, we generate an encounter chart for Forest terrain. On the world gravity chart, the World's size of 6 gives a Gravity DM of +0.

### Tyaeda

Our first type is a producer. On the Native Terrain and Locomotion table, a Roll of 1D gives a result of 2 (Walker). A flux roll on the Ecological Niche table results in a Collector, a mobile photosynthetic creature. We note the A (no) and F (no). On the Quantity table, a flux roll result is +1, meaning a small herd. On the Size table, a Flux roll plus Gravity DM shows 1, or a small creature, Size 3 or approximately 20cm long. Because this is a small creature, we don’t roll on the Strength table, it has a strength of 0. On the Speed table, a roll of 1D with a DM5 produces Walk (5 kph) speed. On the weapons table, a flux roll result of 4 produces spikes. These can inflict nasty cuts to the unwary. For armor, roll high flux produces 2 types of armor. The flux roll for the first type is 2 (1D sealed) the second flux roll is 0 (2D armor). The Tyaeda is a walking (5 kph) forest walker on Empire (Trojan Reaches 2711). It is a small collector. It attacks others with spikes and is defended by armor.

### Citheria

Our second creature is the Herbivore. Rolling 1D on the Locomotion produces a Walker. On the Ecological Niche table, the flux roll is +2 resulting in a forest dwelling Grazer. The reactions are 10  1D or Attack of 8, and a 10  1D or Flee of 5. These are skittish creatures, prone to flee quickly in any encounter. On the Quantity table, a flux roll results in a pair. On the Size table, a flux roll plus Gravity DM has a result of 1 a typical size (size 4) and about 75cm long. As this is a typical sized creature, a roll of 1 with a Mod of 3 results in a strength of 0 On the Speed table, a 1D roll of 4 becomes Charge (30 kph). For the weapon, roll flux +1 for being an herbivore with a result of 1. The creature throws its body around for defense. For the armor, a high flux roll of 1 and the flux roll of 0 results in 2D armor. The Citheria is a charging (30 kph) forest walker on Empire. It is a typical size grazer. It attacks others with its body and is defended by armor.

### Surigmi

The omnivore is our third creature. On the locomotion table, a 1D roll has a result of 6, a flyer. The flux roll on the Ecological Niche table produces a Gatherer. The Attack reaction is 11  1D or 9 and the Flee reaction is 12  1D or 8. The flux roll on the quantity table results in a small group. On the Size table, the flux plus Gravity DM roll is 0 results of typical size (size 4) and 75 cm. A roll of 3 with a modifier of 3 on the strength chart gives a result of 1D3. On the Speed table, a 1D plus a DM +2 for being a flyer has a result of 6, a very fast speed (100kph). The flux roll is 4 on the Weapons table. For this, we choose quills over spikes, but either inflicts cut damage. The high flux roll for armor types produces a result of 0 so no armor protection. The Surigmi is a very fast (100kph) forest flyer on Empire. It is a typical size gatherer. It attacks others with quills and has no armor.

### Gikikushash

The carnivore is the fourth creature. On the Native Terrain and Locomotion table, a roll of 1D produces a walker. On the Ecological Niche table, a flux roll of 3 indicates a pouncer. The reactions for this creature are S and S+ meaning it will attack if it has surprise or flee if it is surprised. On the Quantity table, the flux roll results in a single creature. On the Size table, a flux roll plus Gravity DM gives a result of large (size 5), about 1.5m long. On the Strength table a 1D roll result is 3 giving a Strength of 3D. The Speed 1D roll is 5, a result of Fast (50 kph). For weapons, rolling flux with a 1 DM for carnivore has a result of  1, Teeth, which will do 3D cutting damage. Rolling high flux for armor types has a result of 1. Rolling flux on the Armor table results in 1D Insulated. The Gikikushash is a fast (50 kph) forest walker on Empire. It is a large typical strength pouncer. It attacks others with its teeth and is insulated.

### Kishii

The final creature is a scavenger. On the native terrain and locomotion table, the 1D is 3, a walker. On the Ecological Niche table, the flux for subtype results in Intimidator. The Attack reaction is 12  1D or 11 and the Flee reaction is 11  1D or 10. A flux roll of 1 on the Quantity chart indicates a large group of creatures appears. The flux roll plus Gravity DM result is 2, size 2 very small or about 7.5 cm. # BeastMaker Extended

The BeastMaker extended rules add details to a single creature. There are seven sections for the BeastMaker rules. These are Body Structure, Body Features, Body Size, Trainable, Edible, Life Stages, and Senses. Following the make only as really needed (MOARN) principle, these details are not required for having an encounter (or two) with the creature. You should create the specific details for the Beast only as needed. Creating the extended information is suggested if you intend to create a sophont.

## Body Structure

Physical appearance is determined by body symmetry, the number of limb groups, and location of the braincase and senses. The Body Structure Overview shows the details of the head, torso, limbs, and tail of the sophont. Every animal has a main body, called the torso or trunk which forms the core of the creature. Attached to torso are potentially a head, up to four groups of limbs, and a tail.

### Body Structure Elements

**Symmetry** is the overall layout of the body and limbs. Roll with a DM +2 if Flyer and -2 if Swimmer or Diver.
**Bilateral symmetry** a body has two sides, a left and a right side. Each group of limbs has two limbs, one on the left side the other on the right.
**Trilateral symmetry** has three sides: a right side, a left side and a third one. Each group of limbs has three limbs, one for each side.
**Radial symmetry** body has a layout around a cylinder or sphere and does not have specific sides. Roll 1D for the number of limbs for all the groups of limbs.
**Asymmetric symmetry** has a different number of limbs in each group. Roll 1D for each group of limbs.

### Stance

The orientation of the trunk, and by extension the limbs, of the creature defines its stance.
A **horizontal stance** creature is longer than it is wide or tall. The direction of movement is parallel to the layout of the toros and limbs. Flyers, swimmers, amphibians, and flyphibs tend toward being horizontal stance creatures.
A **vertical stance** creature is taller than it is wide or long. The direction of movement is perpendicular to the orientation of the toros and limbs. Walkers can be either vertical or horizontal stance creatures, and some can alternate between the two stances at need.

### Head

The head is a body structure attached to the front of horizonal creatures or the top of vertical creatures. The head may contain one or more of four important parts of a creatures. If these parts are not in the head, these parts will be in the torso. If the brain and senses are in the torso the creature will not have a head.
**Brain** is the primary thinking and decisionmaking organ.
**Senses** are the primary sense cluster for the creature, including, if they exist, sight, hearing, and smell.
**Breathing** is the opening where the creature breathes. The breathing opening is the source of vocalizations and provides, if the creature has one, the sense of smell.
**Feeding** is the opening where the creature feeds, consuming food. If the creature has teeth, a beak, fangs, or tusks as weapons, the feeding opening is where these exists. Producers may not have a feeding orifice. If both the feeding and breathing opening are in the head or the toros there may be one opening for both.

## Body Features

The body features section gives details about the internal workings of the creature.

### Skeleton

All beasts have an interior support structure, which gives attachment points for limbs and muscles, isolates critical organs, and gives the beast its overall shape.

### Fluids

Biological processes require an interior network of fluid transport. These fluids move digested food, oxygen or other active chemicals, and waste materials, from entrance to exit. The differences between the types are only to provide a description to the players when they injure or kill a beast.

### Skin

Beasts have an exterior covering separating their delicate interior processing from the harsh outside world. The coverings as described here do not provide armor.

### Color

The skin or external covering will have one or more colors. Colors are relative to the eyesight range of the species (see Vision p. 24) and may be different for observers with different visual spectra. Different members of a single species may have different colors based upon gender, caste, life stage, or time of the year.
**Brilliant** colors attract attention, standing out from the general background of their normal habitat. Brilliantly colored beasts are easy to spot and track as they move.
**Bright** colors are also to attract attention, but usually because the environment for the creature is also brightly colored. Colors would include red, orange, and yellow.
**Drab** colors, usually grey, brown, tawny, or blue, serve to hide the beast in their native terrain. These colors can be difficult to spot unless you know what you are looking for. Other colors are possible depending upon the terrain. For example, on ice fields white would be a drab color.
**Camouflage** colors include the drab colors but also add stripes or spots to make spotting them even more difficult. This may include the ability to change color.
**Monochrome** colors are usually found on creatures where vision is not a primary sense, for example, in caves or deep oceans. Colors are usually white or black.

### Breathes

A part of a beast’s life processes requires breathing air, ingesting chemical compounds to aid in continued life processes. If transported to another world, the beast may become sick or die.

**Water**:  The  beast breathes the water or fluid from their homeworld.
**Atm + Water**: The beast can breathe both the atmosphere and the water or fluid of their home world.

## Manipulators

Sophonts manipulate their environment with hands or handlike manipulators. The manipulators also serve as the primary source of the sense of touch. The common forms of manipulators are hands, paws, tentacles, grippers, graspers, and sockets. Front limbs terminate in manipulators, rear limbs do not. Legs, Fins, or Wings in the front limb group have manipulators and are dual use. They can be used as peds, swim aids, or wings. If no limbs have manipulators, assume the mouth is a manipulator.
In addition to the potential manipulators at the end of the front limbs, a beast may possess either a proboscis as an extension of its face or head, or a manipulator tail. Either is treated as a tentacle.
**Grip** is the strength a manipulator adds when holding an object. Grip is a mod for tasks using Strength and involving manipulators.
**Touch** is a modifier to the touch sense action.

**Hand:** Two opposed sets of one or more moderately flexible digits, capable of holding an object.
**Paw:** Several unopposed moderately flexible digits that can grasp and hold an object.
**Gripper:** Two opposed groups of relatively inflexible flaps or digits capable of clamping an object between them.
**Tentacle:** One or more flexible digits capable of entwining or coiling together or in opposition.
**Socket:** A hollow rimmed with muscle capable of holding an object within it, with or without an internal digit.
**Grasper:** Three or more mutually opposed flaps or digits capable of clamping an object between them.

## Animal Training

The ability to train animals has three parts. This section does not apply to sophonts. Defer teaches the animal to defer to a sophont handler. If a beast cannot be taught to defer, it is perpetually wild. Obey indicates the animal’s ability and willingness to learn to obey a sophont handler. Animals that can be taught to defer and obey can be domesticated. Learn Simple Task (LST) teaches the animal one or more tasks.
Roll flux three times on the animal training table to determine the training difficulty for the species. If species trainability is not No, an individual of the species has trainability equal to species trainability plus flux. Each stage of training takes a month, with each additional task training taking another month.
For example, an ivory gazelle rolls Defer with a result of No, Obey with a result of 7D, and an LST result of No. The animal cannot be trained.
For example, a horned groat rolls Defer with a result of 8D, Obey with a result of 7D, and an LST result of 6D. The animal is trainable. The trainer evaluates the animals in the herd, selects one, and applies flux to the three tasks. The Defer for this specific animal (8D + flux of 3) gives a result of 5D, Obey checking (7D + flux of 0) result of 7D, and LST check (6D + flux of 1) result of 5D.

## Size based on characteristics

In the Core Rules Book 3 p. 236-237 are rules for determining the size of a sophont based upon their characteristics. If you are planning on using the details in this section to create a sophont these are alternative rules for generating the size of the creature.
Add the number of dice rolled for the C1 (Str), C2 (Dex), and C3 (End) characteristics, ignoring modifiers. For C2 of Agility or Grace use half the value. With C3 of Stamina use twice the value, and Vigor use half the value.
Creatures with a strength of less than 4D, multi-ply your sum by 12, if the strength is 4D multiply by 48, if the strength is 5D multiply by 60, if the strength is 6D multiply 72, if the strength is 7D multiply by 84, and if the strength is 8D or more multiply by 96. This is the weight of the sophont in kilograms.
Divide the weight in kilograms by the Density of the creature to get the volume in liters. Roll flux to determine one body profile divisor, or if you have already created two, select the smaller value. Divide the volume by 1000, then multiply by the square of the body profile divisor then take the cube root of the product. This is the longest dimension of the sophont in meters. Ap-ply the Body Profile divisors to determine the other two dimensions.

### Animal Endurance

Animal Endurance is the time (in hours) the beast can move at conservative speed (SpeedC) as determined on the Beast Speed chart. Basic Endurance (End) is 1D, Grazers have an Endurance of 2D, Chasers have an Endurance of 1D+2, and Pouncers have an Endurance of 1D2. Sophonts have their endurance, vigor, or stamina determined in the chapter on sophont creation.
A domesticated animal used as a beast of burden can carry a load equal to one-third of its body weight at walking speed (5 kph) for End in Hours.
A domesticated animal, or team of animals, can pull a wheeled wagon or cart (and runners on ice) over level terrain equal to its body weight at walking speed (5 kph) for End in hours.
A trained beast of burden can carry one or more riders with a total load equal to onethird of its body weight at walking speed (5 kph) for End in hours. It is capable of maximum Speed for End minutes every hour or half maximum Speed for 3x End minutes every hour.
Flyers used as pack or riding animals carry half the load of other animals (one-sixth of their body weight). Fliers travel at their Max speed minus 1.

## Food Requirements

All creatures consume food according to their ecological niche. The basic measurement of food energy density is a Calorie. For most creatures a basic requirement is 25 to 30 Calories per kg per day. When active these requirements can be as much as doubled.
An edible animal provides food (meat) equal to one-half of the total animal weight. A marginally edible animal provides meat equal to one-quar-ter of the total animal weight. Edible organs are included in the total available meat. If independent, each is 2% of total animal weight (20 grams per kilogram of animal weight). Edible producers and plants provide 100 – 300 Calories per kg, and creatures provide 300 - 2000 Calories per kg.

### Edibility

Beasts and plants may be suitable for consumption. To determine if this beast, or any other item, is safe to consume roll flux once on the Edibility table. Digestive systems not compatible with humans roll independently on the table.
Edibility of yes means the beast, or other item, may be consumed raw or with minimal preparation. Marginal edibility provides limited nutrition and requires D3 processing steps to become edible. The Process edibility requires 1D+1 processing steps to become edible.
If the beast is edible, roll flux once on the Taste column to determine the taste of the processed result. If the beast is inedible, or not processed properly, roll flux once the consumption column to determine the results.
**Allergen** produces an allergic reaction in the consumer. While rarely fatal unless suffering from other medical issues, the problem will be uncomfortable until medical attention is sought.
**Disease** infects the consumer with debilitating and frequently fatal disease. The disease takes days to weeks to manifest and requires medical attention to treat. In rare cases this disease is transmissible to others.
**Illness** produces Ill effects on the consumer (stomach aches, fever, chills, cramps, etc.) as the item is toxic but not fatally so. Usually, these pass within a few hours, but first aid can ease or eliminate to symptoms.
**No Calories** is a benign lack of reaction, but the consumer derives no calories or other nutrients.
**Parasite** introduces an unexpected guest into the consumer body, which the processing is intended to remove. Parasites may take days or weeks to manifest any symptoms and requires medical treatment.
**Toxic** is quickly fatal to the consumer unless medical treatment is immediately available.

### Processing

Not all food is edible completely raw, especially plants and animals from different biospheres. These may require preparation to become palatable and supply nutrition. The Humans living on Vland, a world noted for the biological incompatibility with humans, developed several processes for the conversion of inedible raw materials into edible and nutritious food. In addition to the process of removing or altering toxins, some preparations are designed to preserve food for longer term storage or used to alter flavors to enhance enjoyment.
Each preparation is termed a recipe, consisting of several methods of treatment listed in order. Thus, a recipe 2C4 involves the processing to remove inedible parts, the addition of a microorganism, and then cooked. An example of this is Bread: Wheat is ground and sifted into flour, yeast (and water) is added, then baked. Among foods native to Vland, recipes of 2B74 and 2BB74 are used for hunted animals and recipes of 984 and 2934 are common for many basic grains.
**Method 1**: Edible raw. The items can be enjoyed consumed unaltered from their source. In many cases these foods can be processed for preservation as raw foods are subjected to the effects of the reducers in the environment. This method is not listed on recipes.
**Method 2**: Basic processing is a usual first step. Beasts are carved up, selecting specific parts for consumption, further treatment, or discarded as inedible. For plants this may involve simply separating the edible parts from the rest or grinding portions of the plant then sifting or filtering the results.
**Method 3**: Aging allows the preparation to sit relatively untouched for days to decades. This allows chemical reactions of various sorts to take place.
**Method 4**: Exposure to heat to cook or bake at temperatures between 100°C and 250°C.
**Method 5**: Exposure to mild heat, sunlight, or UV light. This dries the item, a preservation technique, and the UV may induce some chemical changes.
**Method 6**: Exposure to extreme cold at temperatures of 0°C or colder. This also is a process of freeze drying, where the water freezes into a solid and extracted while the remaining material is left behind.
**Method 7**: Exposure to pressure. Exposure to vacuum extracts volatile chemicals and liquids. Exposure to high pressure induces or enhances some chemical changes.
**Method 8**: Adding brine or salt. For Terran foods this is a preservation technique.
**Method 9**: Treated with an Acid. For Terran foods this is either citric acid (e.g., lemon juice) or vinegar.
**Method A**: Treated with a Base. This neutralizes naturally occurring acids and used as a detergent to remove specific compounds.
**Method B**: Treated with a reagent, catalyst, or enzyme. These nonliving chemicals, sometime extremely complex, introduce chemical changes, remove toxins, or kill parasites from the preparation. Recipes include directions as to the exact items and amounts.
**Method C**: Introduction of a bacteria, yeast, mold, or other microorganism. The use of a living creature to process the food from the inside. Recipes will include a detailed description of which variety to use.
**Method D**: Use of other biological processes. The most common Terran example is the use of bees to process nectar and pollen into honey.
**Method F**: Inedible. Regardless of the number of processing steps this cannot be processed into edible food. Usually this is discarded, though in some cases special handling is required. This method is not listed on recipes but may be noted on descriptions of animals or plants.

# Lifecycle

## Life Stages

Every creature’s life is a succession of life stages, each with its own significance. The life stages of a species determine the traditional lifespan: when an individual begins an adult career, begins to feel the effects of age, expects to retire, and so on. Life stages may also determine   metamorphic changes or shifts in gender or caste.

Life stages are divided into terms. The size of the creature determines the length in standard years of each Term. The life stages chart indicates the number of Terms the sophont spends in each life stage. Beginning with life stage 1, roll Flux for each stage and record the number of terms for each. A value of 0 indicates that the life stage is skipped (or an extremely short duration of perhaps a few weeks). The life stages 1 (child), 5 (peak), and 9 (retired) have a minimum of one term.

**Life Stage 0** is a helpless infant under the care of an adult member of the family. Infancy is a fixed duration, lasting 1/2 a term.
**Life Stage 2** is the period of Gender maturity, and (if the species has Caste) of Caste maturity.
**Life Stage 3** is where career resolution begins.
**Life Stage 5** is when physical aging begins.
**Life Stage 9** is when mental aging begins.

**Life Expectancy** is the sum of years assigned to all the life stages. The effects of the aging rolls determine the life span of an individual.

## Metamorphosis

Metamorphosis is an abrupt and dramatic change in a creature’s physical structure. Meta-morphosis occurs when a creature advances from one life stage to the next. This may happen once, twice, or several times over the lifetime of the creature. Number the successive forms First, Second, Third, (and beyond).
When the change occurs, it produces a new physical structure: create a new body structure to be the next stage: the new form may exhibit no differences, a few differences, or completely new physical and mental characteristics. Depending upon the changes, the process takes between a few days and several months.

## Molting

Molting is the way a creature periodically sheds part or all its outer covering. For feathered or fur coverings, this may be shedding the feathers or fur. For creatures with an exoskeleton or outer shell, this may be the whole outer covering. For skin, scales, or plates, this may be the outer layer of the covering.
Molting takes anywhere from a few minutes to several days or weeks. When completed the subject emerges with a fresh outer covering, per-haps with changed markings and colors.
Molting doesn’t result in any change to physical structure or characteristics.

## Gender

Gender indicates the evolutionarily established reproductive roles within a species and includes physical differentiation between the members of the genders.
There are six genders and five gender structures.
**Solitaire** indicates individuals reproduce without alternate gender mates. This includes both single-gender clone reproduction and hermaphrodite reproduction that requires two (or more) individuals but only one gender.
**Dual** indicates two individuals of different genders pair for reproduction. The two genders are known as Female and Male. Dual gender creatures are called dioecious.
**Female/Male/Neuter** (FMN) indicates there are three distinct genders, but only two, the Female and Male genders, participate in reproduction.
**Egg donor/Activator/Bearer** (EAB) indicates there are three distinct genders, and all have roles in reproduction.
**Group** gender has multiple genders which interact in a complex fashion. Specific structures include a few, some, or all the available genders.

### Assignment

Determine the initial gender assignment by rolling flux on the Gender table. If the gender assignment is at life stage 2, before their assignment the individual has no gender. Gender is randomly assigned at the start of their teen years. By family or By Individual also assigns the gender of the individual at the start of life stage 2, but either the family group or the individual selects the gender. The manifestation of the gender characteristics, including characteristic differences and other physical details, may take weeks to months to fully resolve. Single-gendered creatures are assigned at birth and do not shift.
**Progressive** gender changes at the beginning of each Life Stage in a fixed pattern for each creature. In some cases, the gender may shift to the same gender. The order of gender shifts follows the gender chart for the creature (see form p. 31). If the gender is not assigned until life stage 2, the progressive changes start at life stage 3.
**Transformed** gender creature changes their gender to another (randomly determined) gender once at Life Stage 6. The new gender may be the same as the old gender.

## Reproduction

When a set of sophonts, according to their gender configuration, mate and produce offspring, the biological process of proceeding from mating to offspring has several important details.

### Method of Delivery

Method of delivery determines how the mating of the individuals produces a new member of the species. Roll Flux on the Delivery table to determine their method. Apply a DM 1 if there is only one gender. Apply DM +1 for the EAB genders.
**External budding** creatures have child forms on their surface, growing as a parasite until they reach a specific size then they detach, starting their own independent life.
**Live bearing** has the female, or bearer, carrying the offspring within their bodies where it gestates until they give birth.
**Egg-laying** creatures the female, or egg donor, produces one or more eggs. The egg contains the offspring within a shell where it develops outside of the parents. The birthing process involves the child breaking out of their egg.

### Number of Offspring

Determination of the number of children comes from the number of genders in the species. Roll Flux and cross-reference the number of full genders.
**Litter** determines the number of children by rolling good flux for each mating.
**Child** has one offspring from each mating. In rare cases, there may be two children.
**Multiple** offspring rolls 1D for the number of offspring.
**Many** offspring rolls 1D times 10 for number of children.

### Treatment of Offspring

Every creature treats its offspring differently. Some simply abandon their young to the world, others take time to care for and nurture their young.

### Maturation

Precocious and neotenic are two terms defining how quickly or slowly a creature matures into a full adult. Precocious offspring mature early and are capable of independence faster than expected. Neotenic means keeping the aspects of a child for longer.
Some of this is captured by the life stages. Precocious species will have shorter child, teen, and young adult stages. Neotenic species will have several terms in each of these stages of their lives.
The default for Life Stages 0 (infant) assumes the offspring are helpless and require their parents to care for them. The infants of precocious species are capable of independent actions within a short time, a few hours to weeks rather than months or years. This is especially true for herbivores but can apply to other types as well. The offspring are still dependent on their parents for defense from predators but are otherwise independent.
Select a life stage between 0 (infant) and 4 (adult) for how long the children rely on their parents for food, shelter, and other kinds of care. Solitary creatures or ones in small groups push their offspring away earlier. Beasts that congregate in large groups keep their offspring close for longer. The default for beasts is life stage 1 (child) and for sophonts is life stage 2 (teen).
Selecting life stage 0 means the parents leave the offspring at birth. If the creature is egg-laying, the parents lay their eggs and then leave before the children are born. The offspring either mature early or are left in a place where they will be safe until they can survive on their own.
Selecting life stage 1 means the parents care for the infant but push the child out into the world to survive on its own. Many beasts fall into this category. Their offspring are cared for until they can survive on their own and sent off into the world.
Selecting life stage 2, the general default for most sophont species, the descendants are kept with the family group through gender and caste maturity. This is also the default for most beasts that collect into large groups.

## Genetics

The process of natural reproduction determines the capabilities of the new members of the species. Genetic engineering can change the outcomes of natural reproduction. These changes may be beneficial or detrimental.

### Genetic Inheritance

Characteristics are generated as part of the sophont creation process (Characteristics p. 36) and noted on the sophont creation card. The physical characteristics C1 (Strength), C2 (Dexterity, Agility, or Grace), C3 (Endurance, Stamina, or Vigor), and C4 (Intelligence) are always genetic characteristics. If C5 is Instinct it is also a genetic characteristic, but C5 as Education or Training is not a genetic characteristic. If C6 is Charisma or Social Standing, it is not a genetic characteristic. If C6 is Caste and the caste assignment is by heredity, it is a genetic characteristic, but otherwise not.
Beasts also have genetic characteristics, these are C1 (Strength p. 6), C3 (Endurance p. 13), C4 (Intelligence), C5 (Domestication). The Gene value of Intelligence is either 0 or 1. The Gene value for domestication provides the one die for the flux roll on the animal training table (Training p. 16) that determines how easy the beast is to train.
Characteristics are generated with one or more dice. The first die rolled for a genetic characteristic is the Gene D for that characteristic. The remaining die (or dice), called the Generated D, for the characteristic represent training, experience, and environment. If the sophont generation dictates that a characteristic is rolled with 1D, then that value is the Gene D and there is no Generated D. If the sophont generation dictates a characteristic is rolled with 3D or more, the Gene D is still the first Die rolled.
The values for Genes can be determined during characteristic generation, or later through genetic testing. Ideally, when a new character is created, the first die rolled is the Gene D and should be recorded on the character’s Genetics Card. Genetic testing is a formal process under the guidance of a referee: the player rolls 1D for each genetic characteristic and enters the values on the Genetics Card. The referee takes care to avoid contradictions (such as the Gene D being greater than the present characteristic or the Gene D being less than necessary to produce the present characteristic). Genes for a specific individual are recorded as a six-digit UPP string preceded by DNA, with the nongenetic components shown as X. For example, DNA4456XX.
Genes are inherited from parents and passed on to the offspring. Gene values are also used when creating clones. The Gene value passed to the offspring may increase or decrease because of mutations or genetic engineering. Each Gene will be marked as dominant, standard, or recessive. This selectivity determines which Gene is selected for any children. Each Gene may also be marked as gender-linked or caste linked.

## Offspring

When a set of sophonts, according to their gender configuration, mate and generate a child, the child acquires the Gene characteristic from one of their parents. A Solitaire gender contributes all their Gene characteristics to their children. A Dual gender and FMN gender have contributions from the two parents (neuter gender does not contribute Genes to children). An EAB gender sophont has three parents to contribute to their child. In the group gender, the specifics of which parents contribute to the child depend on the specifics of how the genders in the group interact.
In general, the child receives the Characteristic Gene from one of the parents randomly. A Gene marked as gender-linked may be selected for the same gender children but is never selected for different gender children. Creatures that can select their gender will not have gender-linked genes. A Gene marked as caste-linked may be selected for a same-caste child but never for one of a different caste. If one or more of the parents have a Dominant Gene, these are selected in preference to the Standard Genes. If there are Recessive Genes, the Standard or Dominant Genes are selected over the Recessive ones. Only in the case where all the parents have a matched Recessive Gene are one of the Recessive Genes selected.

### Genetic Changes

Genes can change due to mutation. Mutations can make Genes Dominant, or Recessive, or increase or decrease their values. Roll Flux on the Mutations table when it is transmitted to the offspring, using the results from the standard risk column. A solitary gender rolls twice, the second time using the Solitaire column.
High-Risk individuals are those who were exposed to situations that have a higher likelihood of inducing mutations. They include being subjected to high levels of radiation or hazardous chemicals, starship engineers (because of long term exposure to drive radiation), nonnatives on worlds with tainted atmospheres, and nonnatives on worlds with high energy stars (type O, B, A, and F).
A Gene can be reduced to zero and represents a defective gene or genetically transmitted disease. Genes reduced to below zero represents a lethal mutation. Genes can be increased to 7, but this also represents a defective gene. Genes increased to 8 or more also represent a lethal mutation. Offspring receiving a lethal mutation gene do not survive their birth.
Selective breeding is the process of selecting only the creatures with the best genes for descendants. Several generations of the process can produce some exceptional offspring. Low TL cultures use selective breeding on beasts and plants to enhance their food sources. Selective breeding programs are generally frowned upon when used on sophonts but can occur when a population bottleneck occurs.
In addition to mutation, genes can be edited using a variety of medical techniques. Gene editing (Geneering) is one rationale behind Acquired Characteristic Increases in Character Generation. For a variety of reasons (including game balance), edited Genes are Recessive. That does not prevent such a Recessive from later becoming Standard or even Dominant through various mutations.
In addition to the characteristic changes, the mutations and geneering may make other changes, known as morphological changes, to the organism. There are two stages of morphological changes: minor and major.
Minor morphological changes can occur through mutation, selective breeding, or geneering. These changes are limited to size of the sophont or beast by one size category from the base, speed by one category from the base, or adjusting the senses by changing the constant for each sense by one step from the base.
Major morphological changes occur only through geneering. These changes can alter all parts of the creature, including body structure and features, changing senses constants, addition or removal of senses, and lifespan. Creatures with major morphological changes are chimeras.
While it is possible to engineer a single creature with major morphological changes, they are sterile and cannot produce children. The usual practice is a breeding program of between 50 and 500 individuals, depending on the genders of the base creature, all contributing to the next generation. This allows a stable genetic base for continuing the species. Depending upon the technology level of the engineers, creating a new species can take anywhere from three to 50 or more generations. Once the changes are considered complete, the species can be allowed to breed.

## Uplift

Uplift is the process of enhancing a beast into a new sophont by altering their intelligence and other characteristics. This requires major mor-phological changes. Beasts by default have a C4 (Int) Inherited Gene score of either 0 or 1. So the initial process is to enhance the intelligence of the beast to normal sophont levels. Usually up-lifting adds or enhances manipulators, a voice for communications, and perhaps size to fit in better with existing sophonts.
Examples of uplifted sophonts include Chims, Dolphins, Orangutans, Orca, and Ursa; All uplifted by the Solomani during the First and Sec-ond Imperium area. Because the uplifted crea-tures require major morphological changes, it is correct to refer to these sophonts as either chi-meras or uplifts.

## Clones

A clone is a sophont genetically identical to a single donor parent sophont. A clone is the same gender (and genetic Caste) as the parent. Although a clone begins with the Genes of the one parent, it then rolls Generated dice normally. Thus, clones with the same Genes may exhibit a variety of UPPs. There are five types of clones.
**Natural Clones** occur without the intervention of technology. Identical twins are natural clones (of each other rather than of a parent). Natural clones are birthed as children and proceed naturally through all life stages. Natural Clones have natural organic bodies, natural brains, and naturally formed personalities.
**Offspring Clones** are deliberately created clones intended as children of individuals. They are typically created for an individual driven by a need to have offspring, but who does not care to involve other parents in the creation process. Offspring clones are birthed as children and then proceed through all normal life stages. The offspring of the Solitaire gender are natural offspring clones. Offspring clones are usually the same gender as the parent, though geneering may alter this result. Offspring clones have natural organic bodies, natural brains, and naturally formed personalities.
**Relict Clones** are deliberately created clones intended to replace an existing individual (typically one who is dead or incapacitated). The pattern provides genetic material samples and a personality, memory, and skill recording. Relicts are not created until the pattern has died or disappeared. When a pattern dies, a relict is force grown to Life Stage 3 then implanted with the pattern’s recorded personality, memories, and skills. A relict preserves memory and is an effective duplicate or replacement for the pattern. Relicts have force-grown organic bodies, cloned brains, and implanted personalities.
**Guest Worker Clones** or **Guests** are deliberately created clones for cheap laborers. A suitable pattern provides genetic material samples and a personality/skill recording. Guests are force-grown from genetic material samples and implanted with an edited recorded personality and skills but no memories. A Guest is a skilled duplicate of the pattern, lacking only the memories of the original. Guests have force grown organic bodies, cloned brains, and edited implanted personalities. Guests are typically sterilized when created.
**Medical Clones** or **Meds** are clones deliberately created as reserves of medical replacement parts. When a pattern requires medical repair parts, a Med is force-grown to Life Stage 3 and then used to provide replacement parts. Meds have force-grown organic bodies, cloned brains, and no personalities implanted.

### Generating a Clone

Clones are not exact duplicates of an existing sophont (the pattern) since only the genetics are duplicated. Cloning duplicates the Gene D values of the pattern and dice create the remainder of each characteristic. For each characteristic, the remainder (the Generated D) are rolled normally.
Natural Clones and Offspring Clones mature naturally. They pass through each life stage in real-time. They generate all the characteristics as normal, using the Gene D from the pattern for the genetic values.
Forced growth clones, including Relicts, Guests, and Meds, are born in a metabolic chamber that increases growth at approximately one year of growth per week up to life stage 3. The Forced-Growth process does nothing to increase intelligence or provide a basic personality. The Clone has only the genetic intelligence, and the nongenetic characteristics are all 0. The Clone requires an implanted personality to supply the generated intelligence and other nongenetic characteristics. Forced growth Clones begin physical aging at life stage 4 (one earlier than normal), and mental aging at life stage 8 (one earlier than normal).
Personalities, including the process of creating, editing, and implanting them, are discussed in Core Rules Book 2 p. 253-255.

## Domestication

Domestication is the process of altering wild animals to become docile and trainable through selective breeding. The result is a creature that can interact with sophonts and even enjoy such interaction. The most obvious example is the wild dog domesticated into a household pet.
The basis of domestication lies with genetic changes to the neurological development of the animals. The core changes result in adult animals that retain many of their youthful submissive behaviors and willingness to please the adults. These developmental changes produce other physical changes, notably white or off-color spots and droopy or floppy ears.

# Senses

A beast or sophont perceives the environment through the senses. Each sense concentrates on one of six broad types of phenomena. These are:
**Energy**: The detection of energy is vision. The energy detected is wavelengths of light (which may extend into the infrared or ultraviolet).
**Vibration**: The detection of vibration is hearing. The vibration detected is sound (which may be ordinary sound, infrasonic, or ultrasonic).
**Matter**: The detection of matter is touch. Touch involves contact with objects and sensing of patterns, textures, shapes, temperature, and other information.
**Volatiles**: The detection of chemical (or biochemical) volatiles is smell (in atmosphere); or taste (in solution; typically, water). The sense involves uniquely identifying information. The two are treated as one sense.
**Fields**: The detection of electrical or magnetic fields is awareness. Awareness allows determining the presence or absence of such fields or specific materials relating to them.
**Auras**: The detection of auras is perception. The auras detected are biological (and reflect the presence of life), or sentient (and reflect the presence of thought).

Resolving what and how much is perceived uses sense actions (see Core Rule Book 1 p. 131,187). When senses operate in contact (Touch), Range is ignored, and the action is based on 2D.

## Vision

The sense organ for vision is the eye. It detects radiant energy and feeds it to a nerve system that processes the information. A creature with a sense of vision has eyes and can see. A creature without the sense of vision is blind.

### The Colors of Light

### Seeing Colors

Objects reflect (or radiate) many different colors; creatures can only see the colors their eyes can sense. Colors convey information; creatures seeing different wavelengths harvest different information from what they see.
A white object reflects all colors. A human seeing RGB sees object a white object. A creature seeing PSU also sees a white. A black object reflects no colors. A human seeing RGB sees a black object, as does a creature seeing PSU.
A creature who sees in the Infrared (any of the colors ANIFXZ) can see objects warmer than its body temperature as bright glowing ANIFXZ colors and those colder as dull grey or black regardless of light levels. In addition, the creature can see ordinary objects in reflected ANIFXZ colors. A being with vision in the infrared can see heat: hotspots in machinery, body heat from creatures, or heat traces left by vehicles.
A creature with vision in the ultraviolet can see fluorescing minerals and chemicals left by organic activity. Vision in other colors can detect flaws, document alterations, or overpainting not visible to the original users. The disadvantage of seeing other bands is that some colors may be invisible to the creature. Insignia, markings, color identifiers, warning signs, or alarms may be in invisible colors, and devices may appear to show blank output.

## Hearing

The sense organ for hearing is the ear. It takes in vibration in an atmosphere (gas) or water (fluid) and feeds it to a nerve system that then processes the information. Typically, the ear is sensitive to a range of frequencies that correspond to sound pitch. A creature without the sense of hearing is deaf. Hearing also generates the pitch and range of the creature’s voice. Creatures without a voice are mute.

### The Frequencies of Sound

Freq (F) is the central frequency the ear can hear in Hertz (= cycles per second). F is a power of 2 (so, if F=8, Freq = 2^8 = 256). The difference between any two Frequency values is an Octave.
Span (S) is the number of octaves above and below the Frequency. If Span is 1, then the span of sound the sophont can hear is one octave above and below Freq.
Voice (V) is the central frequency of the creature’s vocal generation. It is measured in Hertz.
Range (R) is the range of octaves of the creature’s vocal generation. If Range is 2, the creature can produce sounds two octaves above and below their voice frequency. A Range of 0 indicates the creature is mute.

### Special Sounds

Some frequencies of sound have additional effects outside of the sense of hearing but do not affect creatures who hear the Frequency naturally.
**Delta Waves** induce or promote sleep. After 5 minutes of exposure, Check C3 (End or ½ Vig or Sta) with failure means the creature falls asleep for 1D minutes the first time; 1D hours the second time.
**Theta Waves** induce hypnotic or trance suggestive states. After 5 minutes, roll Good Flux as a modifier to make suggestions to the subject (see Personals in Core Rules Book 1 p. 181).
**Alpha Waves** induce relaxation states. After 5 minutes, the subject is Sleepy (see Core Rules Book 1 p. 53).
**Beta Waves** promote alertness. After 1 minute of exposure, the subject is Ordinary (see Core Rule Book 1 p. 53) for 1 hour, followed by a return to the previous attention level. Many alarms include output at Beta frequencies.
**Gamma Waves** promote heightened productivity. After 5 minutes, the subject is Optimal (see Core Rule Book 1 p. 53) for 1 hour, followed by a return to the previous attention level.

## Smell

The sense organ for smell is the nose. It gathers molecules in the environment and feeds them to a nerve system that processes the information. Objects emit molecules through evaporation, fragmentation, or combustion providing information about their chemical makeup. Some smells (pheromones) create automatic direct responses that bypass a creature’s consciousness. (See Core Rule Book 1, p. 195)
A creature without the sense of smell is smell blind or anosmic.
Sharpness is the ability to identify increasingly subtle elements of smell and the number of digits in the Universal Odor Profile that a creature may try to identify. Noticing a scent provides the first digit of the profile and the creature may try again to sense the additional digits in the profile subject to Sharpness and Sharpness Mods, stopping when a failure occurs.

### Universal Odor Profile

The Universal Odor Profile identifies scents. Rather than provide a detailed chemical analysis, smells are identified by Primary Smell, an Overtone, and a Nuance (together PON). These three digits define the smell of a scent and how it is perceived by the creature. Smells that share digits of the PON have the same Smell, Overtone, or Nuance. Some smells have up to 3 additional differentiators.

### Racial Scent

Every species emits a characteristic scent (the PON) which uniquely identifies it. Individual creatures have a further three digits to the racial scent to uniquely identify them; the Gender, Caste, and Effect (GCE) digits. All creatures of a specific gender emit the same G scent. If the species has castes, each caste emits the same C scent; otherwise, the C scent is random. The final is the E scent, which is unique to the individual.

## Touch

## Awareness

Awareness senses electrical and magnetic fields. By extension, it senses disturbances in those fields by various masses, creating an ability to sense position and direction. The sense organ for awareness is the nervous system. As a being moves within a field, the nervous system responds to the microcurrents that the field creates, and this information is processed by the brain in much the same the way as skin senses wind or radiant heat.
Acuity defines the number of times the creature may retry using the awareness sense before  deciding  there  is nothing of interest.
Awareness is an analog of Vision, functioning in all directions. As a result, awareness input produces a three-dimensional mental visual image mapped using mind generated false colors They are perceived as transparent colors that do not illuminate or reflect and are perceived despite intervening objects (more or less). To the extent they are not absorbed or attenuated, they create a kind of x-ray vision. Awareness provides three types of sensory input.
Current flowing through wires is sensed as a glow of a specific color (“lek”) even behind walls or barriers (alternating current flickers or pulses). Magnetic objects are sensed as a glow of a different specific color (“mag”).
Awareness detects the background magnetic field of a world that has one and allows the individual to instinctively know magnetic north. A creature away from its homeworld takes one or two sleep cycles for its sense to adjust to the new environment; thereafter, it unfailingly knows local directions.
Massive objects (mountains, buildings, starships) distort local fields and thus register their presence. This is mapped to visual information as hazy, indistinct silhouettes observable even when visually obscured. Awareness senses mountains even when invisible because of forest,  fog,  clouds,  or distance. It senses buildings even in darkness. Larger masses are sensed farther away; metals are more likely sensed than non-metals.

## Perception

Perception senses the auras that surround living things. The auras detected are biological (and reflect the presence of life), or sentient (and reflect the presence of thought). The sense organ for perception is the brain. Native brain structures detect auras associated with life and intelligence directly and process the information.
Perception is an analog of hearing: a creature “hears” the information that the perception sense provides overlaid by brain processes on whatever hearing input is available. As a being moves within a field, the nervous system responds to the microcurrents that the field creates, and this information is processed by the brain in much the same way that the skin senses wind or radiant heat.

### Talents

Talents are described in detail in Core Rules Book 1 p. 168-171. Beasts generally do not have a level for their talent. For sophonts, the individual rolls 2D for the level of their talent at character creation time.

**Chameleon** is the ability to shift the color of the skin or outer layer to match the background. This provides concealment, a Mod to Target Size that reduces the chance that it will be seen. Concealment modifiers provided by Chameleon is a Bad Flux roll, rolled for the entire species.

**Hibernate** is the native ability to enter a short-term or long-term state of reduced consciousness and physical activity. Hibernation is a survival strategy, adapted to by creatures living in an environment where food becomes scarce or disappears entirely for a time. In other cases, the hibernation state occurs when the creature needs to undergo a prolonged healing. For beasts the triggers for entering and exiting the hibernation state are obvious, but never sudden, changes in the environment. Examples include length of day/night cycle or overall temperature. Any wounding which would otherwise kill the beast converts to involuntary hibernation. The creature enters the hibernation automatically, lasting 1D weeks, and emerging fully healed. Upon waking the creature is extremely thirsty and hungry.

**Hypno** is the native ability to create altered mental states in which the subject’s critical thinking faculties are bypassed or overridden. Hypno uses a variety of senses to work its effect but appears to the observer to be extrasensory. Producers and Herbivores use Hypno to persuade predators to avoid eating the creature, Omnivores and Carnivores use Hypno to slow or capture their prey. Trappers and Sirens are classic users of Hypno. Hypno uses the Personals rules, using a strategy of Charming, and tactic of Emotion, and a law of Comfort. This is a 3D check against a target of 10, with a successful check causing the target to either wander off or just stay still for a few minutes.

**Morph** is the native ability to change the shape, contours, appearance, and coloration of one’s body. Morph can extrude additional limbs or enhance existing limbs to fight predators or capture prey. The coloration changes are used for camouflage. In some cases, Morph is used to duplicate another, more dangerous, creature in the same environment. Creatures also use fanciful Morph as a threat display or mating display. The total mass of the body cannot change. Volume cannot change more than plus or minus 10%.

**Poison** means the creature generates a poisonous substance that can wound or kill other creatures. This poison is delivered by the creature’s bite, claw, or spike attack or simply on the skin. Roll Good Flux for the number of dice for the poison attack. A 0D poison, if the attack is effective, has an effect like a rash or inflammation in the target without causing any hits of damage.

**Rage** is the native ability to enter a heightened emotional state characterized by increased physical characteristics, and insensitivity to pain and wounds. At the beginning of any fighting situation a size 5 or larger creature adds 3D hits, a size 4 creature adds 2D hits, and a size 3 creature add 1D hits to their total. Any damage taken by the creature is removed from these Rage hits first. The rage exhausts itself after 1D rounds, after which they are Tired.

**PsiNull** creatures have no psionic capabilities. Their minds and mental processes do not register to others with Psionic ability. Attempts to control their minds have no effect on them and are blank or undetectable to psionic Perception.

**Soundmimic** is the ability to recreate or imitate sounds which the individual has previously heard. Beasts with this ability can produce a wide variety of sounds, including duplicating speech, sounds produced by equipment, and the calls and sounds of other creatures.

### Projector

The projector special talent gives the beast a shortrange projector weapon in addition to their natural melee weapons. This weapon has a range of 1, usually about 3x the length of the creature. For the species roll good flux to determine the dice of damage of the projector, with a mod of 2 for size 4 creature, a 4 for a size 3 creature, and a 6 for a size 2 or smaller creature, +2 for a size 6 creature, and +4 for a size 7 or larger creature, with a minimum of 0D. A 0D damage effects the target, but not enough to cause hits of of an object but causes no real damage and can’t penetrate armor.

An **EMP** projector generates an electromagnetic pulse which can damage or destroy electronic equipment and temporarily blind the awareness sense for 1D turns. Beasts use the blinding effect to escape predators, assuming predators have the awareness sense. Since the pulse can be seen at a much longer distance creatures with awareness may also use the pulse generation as a form of simple communication for alarms, mating, or simple social signals.

A **Flash** projector generates a bright flash of light, usually only with the wavelengths of the visual senses of the creatures of the world, which can blind the visual sense for 1D turns. Like the EMP projector, creatures may use this to deter predators or as a form of communication.

A **Shock** projector generates a strong electric charge which can cause damage to creatures and equipment. Creatures generate an electric charge to stun or kill their prey or to discourage predators. The shock discharge also generates an EMP pulse which can be detected with the awareness sense.

The **Sonic** projector generates a powerful blast of sound which can both inflict damage to creatures and stun them for 1D turns. Creatures use the sonic to stun or kill their prey or to communicate over long distances.

A **Stench** projector generates projects a foulsmelling or obnoxious effect at the target which stuns them for 1D turns. Creatures use the stench projectors to discourage predators, as an alert to drive off competitors, or to attract other creatures. The intensity of the smell matches the size of the creature emitting it.

An **Acid** projector throws a strong acid, doing corrode damage to both creatures and objects. Creatures use this to injury or kill prey. In some cases, the acid serves as part of the beast’s digestion process.

A **Poison Gas** projector generates a cloud of toxic vapors which can both choke an air breathing creature and poison them. A more intense version of the Stench projector, the gas serves to kill prey or discourage predators. Creatures with this projector will be immune to the toxic effects of gas, but not to the suffocation effects and have to hold their breath until the gas dissipates.

A **Fire** projector emits chemicals undergoing a strong exothermic reaction causing burn damage to both creatures and objects and possibly setting things on fire.

### Sound Generation

For creatures with the sound generation ability, in addition to their normal voice and range of sound production they can also produce one of the special sounds. See Core Rule Book 1, p. 193 for a description of these sounds. Effects produced by the sounds take between one and five minutes of exposure.

**Alpha** sounds induce relaxation and lull the target into a state of sleepiness. Used by herbivores or producers to allow escaping from predators, but also used by carnivores to make capturing prey easier.

**Beta** waves induce a state of alertness. Creatures use the sound as part of an alarm or alerting call.

**Gamma** sounds create heightened productivity and a sense of purpose. Creatures that undertake engineering projects like digging dens, building nests use this as part of their vocalizing.

**Delta** waves are a stronger version of the alpha waves, inducing a state of sleepiness, causing the victims to fall asleep for short periods. Used by sirens or trappers to make prey easier to capture.

**Theta** waves induce a hypnotic state and make the listener susceptible to suggestions. Beasts with this ability tend to use simple suggestions like the Hypno talent. Sophonts with this ability get a Good Flux modifier to their use of Personals.

### Scent Generation

The creature generates a pheromone as their racial scent which can affect them, other creatures in their world, and possibly others. This can either be a continuously produced scent or a milder version of the Stench projector.

Normally the pheromones only affect creatures with the same racial PON scent. But other beasts or sophonts with the same letters in their racial PON, through Pseudomone, may also be affected. When the racial PONs share three letters, even in different orders, that is a strong Pseudomone, sharing two letters is an equivalent Pseudomone, and sharing one letter is a weak Pseudomone.

**Scatter** scent is an alarm signal, usually for creatures appearing in a group or herd. The scent produces a fear reaction, and a desire to run. This gives a +2 Mod to the flee roll.

The **Dread** scent marks territories. Like scatter, it creates a fear reaction and a desire to be somewhere else.

An **Alarm** scent is another alarm signal which returns the affected creatures to an Optimal state as they seek out the danger.

**Repellant** scent is a milder form of the Stench, to drive away predators and potential rivals. This gives a +2 Mod to the flee roll.

An **Attractor** scent is a pleasant one, drawing the affected creatures closer to the emitter. This may be to attract mates, pollinators, or by sirens to capture prey.

The **Enhancer** scent returns the affected creature to an Ordinary state, allowing them to work more effectively on tasks as hand.

A **Courage** scent encourages the beast to attack rather than flee. This gives a 2 mod to the Flee roll and a +2 mod to the Attack roll.

The **Smoother** scent calms fears and returns the creatures to a clam and restful state of mind, returning the affected creature to and Ordinary state.

### Psi Talent

For beasts that have evolved to the edge of sentience, but not quite at full sophonts, it is possible for some to use a very limited form of PSI powers. Beasts will not have a full range of PSI abilities and do not need to go through the training. In the future, as the creature evolves into a full sophont they gain access to the full set with an affinity for the talent of their ancestors.

Psionic beasts roll to allocation their Basic Ability scores. Roll 2D to determine their Direct/Self ability, 1D to determine their Remote ability, and 2D to determine their skill with the specific talent. These rolls are for the entire species for simplicity, though if the referee wants to surprise their players, they can add a flux roll to determine exact ratings for an individual. This gives a basic idea of how effective the beast will be using their one talent. Using talents is tiring and psi-active beasts can use their talent up to 1D times per personal day before becoming sleepy and require rest.

**Teleport** allows small creatures to teleport short distances. This is usually an instinctual process to avoid a predator or to capture prey, but occasionally to get into or out of places they could not otherwise Creatures can teleport distances up to their Remote ability and limited by their ability to see or sense their destination. Creatures of size 2 require a total of Self plus their teleport skill of 12 or more. Creatures of size 3 require a total of Self plus teleport skill to be 20 or more.

**The Touch** allows the creature to heal themselves. Normally any PSI creature can heal one hit, if their total of Self and their touch skill 12 or more, they can heal two hits, and if the total is 20 or more, they can heal three hits. Normally creatures with the Touch will heal only themselves but can be trained to heal others.

Energy shifting or **EShift** in creatures has one of two effects. The first is altering their internal temperatures to make themselves more comfortable or as a defensive reaction. Normally creatures only shift temperatures in one direction (colder or warmer) depending upon circumstances. While EShift can heat or chill objects to the point of doing damage, most beasts won’t hurt themselves. The second effect is throwing small object by imparting a velocity on them. Usually these are small (10 gram) stones or other objects and thrown at 1 to 3 meters per second. This effect is used to stun small prey or surprise potential predators. These won’t do hits of damage to normal sized creatures.

**Move** allows creatures to move other objects or themselves by the power of their mind. Creatures use this as an extra limb usually at a meter or less. A few learn to reach up to about 5 meters, but that’s usually their limit. Any PSI creature can pick up and move objects of size 1, limited to 10  grams of mass. If their total of Direct and move skill is 11  or more, they can pick up and move objects of size 2, around 75 grams of mass. If the total of the Direct and move skill is 20 or more, they can move objects of size 3, around 1 kg of mass. For small creatures where their Self plus move skill allows them to lift their own mass, they can fly.

The **Cloud Minds** power projects a “not my problem” message with the practical effect of invisibility. This works only up to about 5 meters. It works on size 1 creatures, or size 2 creatures if their total of self and cloud skill is 11 or more, or size 3 creatures if their self plus cloud skill is 20 or more.

Beasts concentrate their **psi-sense** into one sense. Roll flux on the Sense table to determine the constant for their PSI sense and select one of the senses to apply this to. Beasts will use the same sense descriptions as their natural senses.

**Empathy** gives the ability to both sense and project emotions. Predators use empathy to lull their prey into a false sense of security and calm. Prey animals use empathy as an alarm call. Social animals use empathy to project simple thoughts about their state of being. The sense and projection effects are limited to around 5 meters.

**Mentation** is the ability to control other creatures and make them do whatever is asked. The effect works at 5 meters or less. With an empathy skill plus Self of 8 or more can affect creatures of size 2, 13 or more affect creatures of size 3, 20 or more affect creatures of size 4.

## Sophont Abilities

If the special ability is one of the Arts skills or one of the Trade skills, everyone of that species, gender or Caste gets that skill with a level equal to their Int. This skill is in addition to any other skills obtained during character generation. The level of skill may be increased during their career. If the result on the Trades column is “Skill”, roll once on the Caste Skill Table (p. 40) to determine which skill gained.

If the special ability is Talents, the talents and their use are described in Core Rules Book 1, p. 168171. The individual rolls 2D for the level of their talent at character creation time. The result of Mem Sense gives an eidetic memory for one of their senses. Select or determine which active sense is affected.

Insight and Curiosity are two of the Intuitions, and described in Core Rules Book 1, p.180. The individual will always have either Insight or Curiosity for each session.

If the special ability is one of the Senses, and the species lacks the sense, the species, gender, or Caste now gains the sense. Determine the sense string. If the species has the sense, add +2 to the sense constant.

## Symbionts

Symbiosis is two unlike creatures living together. There are many forms of symbiotic relationships but here we discuss an endosymbiosis where a sophont (called the Rider) lives within or on an-other creature (called a Carrier). The carrier may or may not be a sophont and provides the physical characteristics for combined creature.

A Dominated Carrier is where the Rider has con-trol of the carrier creature. The rider provides the Int and C5 characteristics for the combined creature.

An Assisted Carrier is where the Carrier is also intelligent and the two work together, sharing their experiences, to solve problems.

Depending on the nature of the symbiotic relationship, it may or may not be possible for the Rider to be transferred to another Carrier. An obligate symbiosis means the Rider cannot survive outside of the Carrier and separating them may kill one or both. A non-obligate symbiosis mean the Rider can survive away from the Carrier and be transferred to another Rider.

# BeastMaker Extended Example

For our extended example, we select the Surigmi, the omnivorous flyer from the basic example on p. 10. The extended features rely heavily on flux-based dice rolls. Unless otherwise specified, all dice rolls are flux rolls.

The Surigmi is a very fast (100kph) forest flyer on Empire. It is a typical size gatherer. It attacks others with quills and has no armor. Surigmi are encountered in small groups.

## Body

To start with the body structure, make five rolls. The first is for symmetry: a roll of 1 results in bilateral. For the head and torso, a roll of 3 produces an overall structure of a head with senses, and the torso contains the brain. A roll of 1 for the front limb groups has a result of WL. The front limb group has one set of wings and one set of legs. The bilateral symmetry gives each group a pair of limbs, so two wings and two legs. For the rear limbs, the roll of 1 has a result of LN, meaning there is one set of legs, or two legs. The final roll of 2 determines the Surigmi has a tail.

Determining body features requires five rolls. The first is for the skeleton, where a roll of 0 results in a bony interior. A roll of 3 for fluids has a result of hemolymph, copper-based blood in an open circulatory system. For skin, a roll of 3 is a result of a furry pelt. Combined with the presence of the quills as a defensive mechanism, we decide the quills are a modified hair shaft. For color, a flux roll of 0 gives a drab color. The last roll of +2 gives paws. On a final note, as a flyer, the Surigmi breathes the air of its homeworld, a standard atmosphere.

The size of the Surigmi starts with the base size (75cm). Rolling once on the variation size (1) has a result of 70cm. From the stance rules, this is the height of the Surigmi. The first roll on the divisor table is 2, or a divisor of 11 and a depth of 6.4cm. The second roll on the divisor table is 3 for a divisor of 6 and a length of 11.6cm. Multiplying these values together gives a total volume of 5.2 liters. The density roll is 4, for standard density, and a final weight of 5.2 kg.

### Training

We determine if the Surigmi can be domesticated and trained with three rolls. The first roll of 4 gives a result of 4D (a Formidable task) to defer to the trainer. The second roll of 3 gives a result of 5D (a Staggering task) to learn obedience. The third roll of 0 gives a Learn Simple Tasks of 8D (a beyond Impossible task). The Surigmi are difficult to train and use their quills to discourage most efforts.

### Edibility

A roll of 0 shows they are inedible to humans.

### Life Stages

For the Surigmi, the base size of 4 gives each term of their life as 2.6 years. Their infancy lasts for 1.3 years. After nine rolls of 4, 3, 2, 1, 0, 4, 2, 1, and 0 we end up with the number of terms for each stage as 4, 1, 3, 2, 2, 4, 3, 2, and 2. The total for this is 23 terms or 60 years.

### Gender

Determining the Surigmi genders a roll of 2 indicates EAB; three genders, all are involved in reproduction. A roll of +1, with a DM+1 for the EAB gender shows Surigmi are egg layers. A roll of 2 indicates genders are determined at birth. A roll of 1 shows genders are fixed for life. A final roll of 2 gives offspring of a single child, a single egg each breeding season. The Surigmi cares for their child for their extended (11 year) childhood.

## Senses

The next step is determining the senses by making five rolls on the senses table. The first roll of 1 indicates vision. The second roll of 5 indicates hearing. The third roll of 1 indicates anosmic, the Surigmi has no sense of smell or taste. All creatures, including the Surigmi, have a sense of touch. A roll of 1 shows awareness, and a final roll of 4 shows oblivious, or no sensitivity to auras. The abbreviated sense string is VHTA.

Empire's primary determines the visual bands of the Surigmi. In this case, a G2 V star gives a base RGB for color vision. A roll of 1 gives a constant of 18. The final vision string is V18RGB.

Hearing and voice have six parts. A roll of 1 gives a hearing constant of 14. A roll of 4 for frequency indicating a central value of 1,000 Hz. A second roll of 2 gives a span of 4, or 64 Hz to 16,000 Hz. A roll of 2 gives a voice frequency of 8, around middle C and in the lower half of the hearing range of the Surigmi. A roll of 1 gives a range of 1 for the voice. This gives the final hearing string of H14A481. The last part is the type of voice, where a roll of 4 determines the Surigmi uses whistling as its voice.

The Touch constant for a roll of 2 is 12 and the sensitivity from a roll of 1 is 3. This is a touch string of T123.

The final sense is awareness. A roll of 0 gives a constant 16, a roll of 1 gives an awareness acuity of 3, and the awareness string of A163.

# Sophont Creation

The Sophont Creation System provides a variety of nonhuman characters to populate your universe. Each use creates a single unique intelligent species for Traveller, which can be used as player characters. The process does not create a character: it records the information needed to later create one or more characters with this species.

Sophont creation is based on two far-reaching assumptions. First, intelligent creatures exist in roughly the same planetary environments as humans. They breathe atmospheric gases (or water) normally found on human-inhabited worlds and they can live in roughly the same environments. Second, sophonts created by this system live within the greater social and economic structure of interstellar society; they compete with everyone else (including humans) for jobs and positions in that society. Members pursue careers in the same way that any human character does.

If you intend to create an intelligent species, but not one for use as a player character or one that does not match the assumptions above regarding the creature's home environment, the rules in this chapter are optional. Like the BeastMaker Extended chapter, these rules provide details for a single species.

## Identifying a Sophont

It’s easy to create a name for a species of So-phonts or beasts (no more than a random word); the standard reference to any specific Sophont is the Sophont Long Name: the species name of the Sophont along with the home world name and its home star and orbital data.

For example,
The Solomani of Terra (Sol 3 \[G2 V])
The Aslan of Kusyu (Tyeyo 3 \[G4 V])
The Urdushkha of Irdi (Irluush 5 \[F4 V])

Some worlds have a substantial population of non-native sophonts. Where they live as a group on a world, they are identified as “from” if they are not on their current home world. For example, there is a substantial Terran settlement on a world orbiting Vega: They are identified as The Solomani from Terra (Sol 3 \[G2 V])

## The Standard Environment

Most life forms prefer to exist within a narrow range of environmental settings which they consider their standard livable habitat. The standard which suits Humaniti also suits many species and is familiar to most players.

The standard environment has a gravity between 0.75G and 1.25G. An oxygen-nitrogen atmosphere with an atmospheric pressure be-tween 0.5 and 2 atmospheres with oxygen be-tween 15 and 50 percent with the remainder being other inert gasses. Temperatures range be-tween 0°C and 25°C. The atmosphere limits exposure to ultra-violet light or more energetic forms of radiation.

Sophonts from Hot, Tropic, Cold, or Frozen worlds or breathing dense or thin atmospheres will find the other end of the standard environment to be uncomfortable and may require special gear to survive there for long.

## Rates of Native Intelligent Life

The Referee needs to decide how common sophonts are in their universe. Worlds with an atmosphere type 29 or DEF may have native life forms. Worlds with an atmosphere of type ABC may have exotic natives. See Core Rules Book 3 p. 28 to determine if a world does have life forms.

Universes where sophonts are rare will have an average of one species per sector. An average balance will have 1 sophont per subsector, or 15 to 20 per sector, with an equal number of proto-sophonts or extinct species. In a rich universe, half of the worlds may host, have hosted, or potentially host an intelligent species.

C2. A sophont may have Dexterity (hand-eye coordination), Agility (body coordination), or Grace (body-limb coordination).
C3. A sophont may have Endurance (resistance to fatigue), Stamina (long term task persistence), or Vigor (short term task persistence).

## Caste Descriptions

These are more detailed description for each of the possible castes. The castes are for each of the possible types except Skilled castes. These are, by design, broad and generic. When the system is completed the descriptions of the individual castes can be expanded for the specific culture.

### Base Castes

**Muscle**, **Laborer**, **Breadwinner**, **Soldier**, and **Unit** are the base caste, the workers filling all the required niches of a functioning society not covered by the other castes.

**Brain**, **Director**, **Archon**, **General**, and **Ruler** are the leader caste. Members serve in the top leadership positions in the culture.

## Structure Castes

### Body

**Healer** is devoted to caring for others, serving as doctors, nurses, and other medical professionals.

**Antibody** is the police force, working to both enforce laws within the culture and defending the civilians from external threats.

**Sensor** observe events to forecast future events as scientists and researchers.

**Memory** serve as cultural memory as records keepers, librarians, and historians.

**Voice** are communications experts, serving as messengers, diplomats, negotiators, and entertainers.

**Claw** are the combat specialists, generally for combat against external threats, but also other members of their own species.

### Economic

**Innovator** design new technological items, or new ways of doing things.

**Guard** is the police force, working to enforce laws within the culture and defending the civilians from external threats.

**Researcher** is charged with creating new scientific discoveries.

# Sophont Creation Example

This example of the combined Beastmaker and Sophont Creation system builds the Vegans of Muan Gwi (Solomani Rim 1717). The example describes the Vegans in terms of the creation systems, including their use as a possible player character. In addition, the example demonstrates the use of the creation system as an inspiration. There are no dice rolls used during the generation process; items are selected to match the canon description.

The Vegan is a Sprinting (20kph) desert walker on Muan Gwi (Solomani Rim 1717). It is a large size, typical strength omnivore gatherer. It uses its body as a weapon and has no armor. It is usually found in a pack.

For a body form, Vegans are bilateral, with a head containing senses and brain. The front limb group has one set of arms. The rear limb group has one set of legs. They have no tail. The arrangement of the limbs makes them upright (vertical stance). The body has a bony interior and leathery skin. Interior fluids are humours. The manipulators are tentacles.

For size, the Vegans are described as 2.2m tall (size 5.4) and 140kg. By calculating the divisors, we arrive at a width of 36 cm (divisor of 6) and a depth of 18 cm (a divisor of 12).

At standard density the Vegan weight matches the de-scription.

For senses, the Vegans have vision, hearing, smell, and touch, but no awareness or perception. The Vegan primary is F2 V, giving a vision range of FIN covering the near to far infrared. The final vision string is V-18-FIN. The hearing string is H-14-6362, Vegans hear over a frequency range of 8 Hz to 512 Hz with their standard vocal range covering 16 Hz to 256 Hz. Vegans can converse with Humans only if they talk in their highest-pitched voices and can hear only the lower half of Human speech. Smell is S-12-2, some-what less than Human standards. The racial scent is V4D-(CT)55. Touch is T-18-3, somewhat more sensitive than a Human’s.

Vegans have two genders, and an individual’s gender is determined at birth and fixed for their lifetime. They have a single child for each birth. The Vegan lifespan is over two hundred years. The corresponding aging chart contains 6 terms per life stage.

Characteristics for Vegan characters are Strength, Dexterity, Endurance, Intelligence, Education, and Social Standing (SDEIES). Vegans do not have castes.
