# Space Travel Rules

## Jump Travel

### HR-ST-001 — Misjump Severity

**Status:** APPROVED  
**Source:** Combined Classic and Mongoose rules  
**Rule:** When a misjump occurs (failed Astrogation check or poor fuel quality), roll 2d6 on the following table:

| Roll | Severity |
|---|---|
| 2-3 | Catastrophic: Ship emerges 1d6 parsecs off course in random direction; takes 2d6×10% hull damage |
| 4-5 | Severe: 1d3 parsecs off course; 1d6×10% hull damage; power plant offline 1d6 days |
| 6-8 | Moderate: 1d3-1 parsecs off course; minor system damage |
| 9-11 | Minor: Correct destination but arrive at edge of system (1d6 days extra travel) |
| 12 | Lucky: No navigational error but 1d6 days late |

### HR-ST-002 — Jump Masking

**Status:** APPROVED  
**Source:** Official clarification + house adaptation  
**Rule:** Ships must be at least 100 diameters from any significant gravity source to jump safely. For gas giants above 100,000 km diameter, use 200 diameters. Jumping closer increases misjump risk (DM-2 per 10 diameter increment within threshold).

## Shipboard Life

### HR-ST-010 — Crew Morale

**Status:** OPTIONAL  
**Source:** Referee design  
**Rule:** For campaigns with a persistent crew, the referee tracks a Crew Morale score (starting at 7). Events can raise or lower it. When Morale drops below 4, crew members may demand higher wages, take independent action, or leave. When Morale reaches 10+, crew may perform beyond normal expectations (DM+1 to key ship actions).

## Encounters

### HR-ST-020 — Piracy Encounter Reaction

**Status:** APPROVED  
**Source:** House rule for Eclipsed Horizons setting  
**Rule:** When encountering pirates in the Eclipsed Horizons subsectors, the referee first rolls 2d6 for pirate disposition (2-4: aggressive attack, 5-7: demand to heave to and be boarded, 8-10: attempt to negotiate a toll, 11-12: mistake players for someone else — potential adventure hook). Player Broker or Persuade checks can shift disposition by 1 step.
